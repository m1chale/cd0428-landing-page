Parkstein

    Location

    Parkstein (Bavarian: Boakstoi or also Berchstoi) is a market in the Upper Palatinate district of Neustadt an der Waldnaab. The village is surrounded by many forests and fields. At an altitude of over 541m above sea level, the area of the municipality covers 30.84 km2.

    Innerhalb von Parkstein gibt es 14 Gemeindeteile und folgende Nachbarorte: Stadt Weiden, Stadt Pressath, Gemeinden Schwarzenbach, Kirchendemenreuth, Altenstadt a.d.Waldnaab.

    Population

    For many years, Parkstein had an increasing number of inhabitants, which could be explained by the designation of building areas in a scenic location, by the immediate proximity to the city of Weiden and especially by the settlement of the expanding Winkler group of companies (Witron, FAS, etc.). Since 2000, the population development has stagnated.

    Currently, about 2300 people live in the municipality.

    History

    Parkstein was probably settled around the year 1000. According to legend, an imperial hunting party had noticed the beautiful basalt cone while pursuing a boar, so one of the hunters decided to build Parkstein Castle there. The castle at the site was first mentioned in a document in 1053. In 1052 Duke Konrad of Bavaria burned down the castle. Emperor Heinrich IV had the imperially owned castle rebuilt at the end of the 11th century. Parkstein subsequently had many owners, including Emperor Frederick Barbarossa and the Wittelsbach dynasty. Several times, as was common in the Middle Ages, the village was mortgaged.

    Characteristics

    Parkstein is located around the basalt cone of the same name of volcanic origin in the nature park. But do not worry the volcano is no longer active for a long time. However, it leaves a unique sight, which attracts many visitors and tourists.
